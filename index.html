Platform

A relevant concern with online studies is whether each response was submitted by a single attentive human participant.

This breaks down into three specific concerns:

Attention

Whether participants pay attention to study materials

AI and Bots

Whether responses are generated by AI agents, LLMs, or Bots

Account Fraud

Whether multiple responses were submitted by the same human using multiple accounts or fake accounts

Each of these concerns can be addressed with targeted quality checks. This dashboard tracks the checks introduced and validated in our companion paper — and will expand as researchers contribute new metrics.

References

Builds on “Mission Possible: The Collection of High-Quality Data” by Çelebi, Exley, Harrs, Kivimaki, Serra-Garcia & Yusof (2026). The paper provides an evidence-based assessment of data quality across online survey platforms, laboratory benchmarks, and AI agent responses. Materials are available in the companion GitHub repository.

Data Quality Metrics

Attention Check Rate Attention

The share of participants who pass attention checks embedded in the survey — such as instructed response items (e.g., “Please select ‘Strongly disagree’ for this question”), infrequency probes, or reading comprehension questions.

Higher is better — higher pass rates indicate more attentive participants.
AI / LLM Detection Rate AI and Bots

The share of responses identified as likely generated by an AI system (e.g., large language models or chatbots) rather than a genuine human participant. Detection relies on behavioral fingerprinting, text analysis, and platform-specific tools.

Lower is better — lower rates indicate more authentic human responses.
Account Fraud Detection Rate Account Fraud

The share of responses linked to fake accounts, duplicate submissions, or coordinated inauthentic behavior — cases where a single person submits multiple responses under different accounts, or where accounts are created fraudulently.

Lower is better — lower rates indicate more genuine participants.
